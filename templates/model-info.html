Kepler / KOI feature reference

Short descriptions of each KOI (Kepler Object of Interest) feature and why it matters for a machine learning model that predicts whether a signal is a real exoplanet transit or a false positive.

Feature Meaning Why it can affect ML prediction
koi_period Orbital Period () Orbital period of the candidate (days). True planets produce consistent, repeating dips at a fixed period; irregular or non-repeating periods often indicate noise or transient events.
koi_period_err1 (Orbital Period Error (Upper) Positive uncertainty of the period estimate. Large uncertainty reduces confidence in a periodic signal; models learn that low error is more planet-like.
koi_period_err2 (Orbital Period Error (Lower)) Negative uncertainty of the period estimate. Same role as koi_period_err1 — asymmetry in errors can signal measurement issues or poor model fits.
koi_time0bk_err1 (Transit Epoch (BKJD) Error (Upper)) Positive uncertainty on the epoch/time-of-transit reference (backwards/benchmark). Uncertain transit epoch weakens the case for regular timing; ML uses this to penalize poorly-timed events.
koi_time0bk_err2 (Transit Epoch (BKJD) Error (Lower)) Negative uncertainty on the epoch/time-of-transit reference. Helps gauge reliability of transit timing; large error ranges suggest low confidence signals.
koi_time0_err1 (Transit Epoch (BJD) Error (Upper)) Positive uncertainty on the first-transit time estimate. If the reference transit time is imprecise, the periodicity test is weaker — bad for planet classification.
koi_time0_err2 (Transit Epoch (BJD) Error (Lower)) Negative uncertainty on the first-transit time estimate. Same as koi_time0_err1 — asymmetric errors can expose fitting problems or data gaps.
koi_impact (Impact Parameter) Impact parameter (0 = central transit, ~1 = grazing transit). Transit shape depends on impact parameter; extreme values (very grazing) can mimic partial eclipses or be poorly fit by planet models.
koi_duration (Transit Duration) Duration of the transit (typically in hours). Duration combined with period and stellar size must obey orbital dynamics; unrealistic durations flag false positives.
koi_duration_err1 (Transit Duration Error (Upper)) Positive uncertainty in transit duration. High uncertainty reduces confidence in the transit shape; ML treats reliable durations as stronger planet indicators.
koi_duration_err2 (Transit Duration Error (Lower)) Negative uncertainty in transit duration. Same as koi_duration_err1 — used to assess how well the transit is measured.
koi_depth (Transit Depth) Depth of the transit (often in ppm — parts per million). Depth indicates planet-to-star size ratio; very deep signals are often stellar eclipses, not planets. Critical for distinguishing planets from binaries or noise.
koi_prad (Planetary Radius) Estimated planet radius (in Earth radii). Unrealistic radii (too large for given depth & star) or values matching stellar companions point to false positives. Helps the model learn plausible planet sizes.
koi_prad_err1 Planetary Radius Error (Upper) Positive uncertainty on planet radius. Radius uncertainty affects confidence: large errors reduce trust that the candidate is a reasonable planet size.
koi_sma (Orbit Semi-Major Axis) Semi-major axis (astronomical units, AU) — orbital distance estimate. Links period, duration, and stellar properties; inconsistent sma relative to period/duration suggests wrong modeling or non-planetary origins.
koi_insol_err1 (Insolation Flux Error (Upper)) Positive uncertainty on insolation flux (stellar energy received). High uncertainty means poor constraints on physical context (habitability, equilibrium temperature) and can signal poor parameter estimation.
koi_insol_err2 (Insolation Flux Error (Lower)) Negative uncertainty on insolation flux. Same as koi_insol_err1 — used by models to judge physical plausibility of the candidate.
koi_model_snr (Transit Signal-to-Noise Ratio) Signal-to-noise ratio of the fitted transit model. High SNR typically means a clean, detectable transit; low SNR events are noisy and harder to trust as planets.
koi_num_transits (Number of Transits) How many transits were observed in the light curve. Multiple consistent transits (≥3, commonly) strengthen planet classification. Single-transit detections are ambiguous and more likely to be false positives or long-period planets.
koi_bin_oedp_sig (Odd-Even Depth Comparison Significance) Metric / significance that indicates an eclipsing binary (OEDP = odd-even depth / other binary-diagnostic tests). Directly flags binary-star-like behavior (e.g., alternating deep/shallow eclipses). A high value suggests a false positive due to an eclipsing binary.
koi_srad (Stellar Radius) Stellar radius (in solar radii). Transit depth scales with (Rp / Rstar)^2. Without stellar radius, depth and planet radius are ambiguous — stellar context is essential for physically meaningful predictions.

Short ML guidance: combine timing & periodicity features (period, epoch errors, num_transits) with shape features (depth, duration, impact) and context (stellar radius, sma, prad, SNR, binary diagnostics). Also use the error/uncertainty columns as confidence signals — the model can learn to down-weight uncertain measurements.
